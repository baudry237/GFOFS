# 🧬 GFOFS Crisis Cart
## Ultra-Low Risk Foods for Severe Gut Flare Phases

This document lists **only foods already present in the GFOFS classification tables** that have **LOW values across all available markers**.

The Crisis Cart is designed for periods of **severe gut instability**, including:

- IBS flare
- SIBO flare
- histamine reactions
- early celiac gut recovery
- unexplained digestive instability

The goal is **maximum digestive predictability with minimal variables**.

# Selection Criteria

Foods included must meet the following:

• Gluten-free  
• Low FODMAP  
• Whole / minimally processed  
• **Low lectins**  
• **Low fiber (preferred)**  
• **Low histamine content**  
• **Low histamine interaction**  
• **Low fat load**

Only foods already present in the **GFOFS classification tables** are considered.

# Nutrient Coverage Warning

The Crisis Cart diet is intentionally restrictive and should be used **temporarily**.

### Nutrients generally adequate

- Protein  
- Vitamin B12  
- Iron  
- Selenium  
- Potassium  

### Nutrients that may require attention

- Vitamin C  
- Calcium  
- Magnesium  
- Zinc  
- Vitamin D  
- Omega-3 (EPA/DHA)  
- Iodine  
- Folate  

### Practical solutions

- Eat fish regularly or consider fish oil for omega-3
- Use **iodized salt** to maintain iodine intake
- Consume papaya or blueberries regularly for vitamin C, or consider vitamin C supplementation
- Consider magnesium, calcium, and zinc supplementation
- Consider vitamin D supplementation
- Optional low-dose methylfolate if folate intake is low

## Constipation / Motility Support (IBS-C)

Because the Crisis Cart diet is intentionally low in fiber, some individuals may experience constipation.

If constipation occurs, consider the following gentle options:

Preferred options:
- magnesium supplementation (200–400 mg, evening)
- psyllium husk (5–10 g/day)
- adequate hydration
- regular movement and walking

Optional natural motility aid:
- kiwi fruit (1–2 per day if tolerated)

Avoid stimulant laxatives such as senna or bisacodyl unless medically directed.

# Important Use Note

The Crisis Cart is **not meant as a long-term diet**.

It is designed to:

1. Stabilize digestion
2. Reduce inflammatory triggers
3. Simplify food variables

Once symptoms improve, foods should gradually be reintroduced from the **main GFOFS classification tables**.
