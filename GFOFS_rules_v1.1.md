# GFOFS_rules_v1.1.md
## Gut Food Optimized Filters Set — Rules

This document defines the authoritative ruleset for the Gut Food Optimized Filters Set (GFOFS).  

---

## 1. Core Principles

GFOFS is designed for individuals with:
- Celiac disease
- IBS or functional gut disorders
- High gut sensitivity during flare or recovery phases

The framework prioritizes gut stability, reproducibility, and minimal variables.

---

## 2. Hard Filters (Exclusionary)

The following filters are **mandatory**. Foods failing any hard filter are excluded.

### 2.1 Gluten-Free
- No gluten-containing grains or derivatives
- No cross-contaminated products

### 2.2 Low FODMAP
- Foods must be low FODMAP at standard serving sizes
- Known high-FODMAP foods are excluded

### 2.3 Prolamines
- Only foods with low or acceptable prolamine content are allowed

---

## 3. Processed Food Exclusion

- Ultra-processed foods are excluded
- Bottled sauces, dressings, desserts, and processed mixes are excluded
- Additives, emulsifiers, yeast extracts, and flavor enhancers are excluded

Whole, single-ingredient foods are preferred.

---

## 4. Informational Filters (Non-Exclusionary)

These filters **do not exclude foods** but provide context for individual tolerance.

### 4.1 Fiber
- Fiber content is tracked for informational purposes
- Fiber tolerance is individual and phase-dependent

### 4.2 Lectins
- Lectins are tracked as a **risk indicator only**
- They do not exclude foods by default

### 4.3 Fat Load
- Fat content is informational
- High fat load may affect tolerance depending on individual and phase

### 4.4 Histamine (Content)
- Histamine is treated as an informational filter
- Foods are not excluded based on histamine content alone

Foods labeled as freshness-dependent are low in histamine when fresh but may accumulate histamine over time due to storage, refrigeration, freezing/thawing, or aging. Cooking does not remove histamine once it has formed.

### 4.5 Histamine Interaction
Some foods may trigger symptoms in sensitive individuals **without containing much histamine** by promoting histamine release or histamine-related reactions.

- This is tracked as a **separate informational column**: **Histamine interaction**
- It does **not** exclude foods by default
- Values are standardized for consistency and sorting:

**Histamine interaction values**
- **Low**: not commonly associated with histamine-release reactions
- **Moderate**: possible trigger in sensitive individuals; inconsistent tolerance
- **High**: common trigger pattern reported by sensitive individuals

**Important:** “Histamine (Content)” and “Histamine interaction” are intentionally separate.
- **Histamine (Content)** = how much histamine the food typically contains (and how storage may increase it)
- **Histamine interaction** = how likely the food is to provoke histamine-related symptoms independent of content

---

## 5. Food Categories

Foods are classified into clear categories, including:
- Animal proteins
- Vegetables (non-legume)
- Fruits
- Fats and oils
- Herbs and spices

All categories are evaluated using the same GFOFS filters.

---

## 6. Interpretation Rules

- “Allowed” does not mean universally tolerated
- Individual response and symptom phase matter
- During active symptoms, conservative choices are recommended
- During remission, tolerance may expand
